Disclaimer

A winlink net is an amateur radio net, which members check in regulary, in our case once a week, to create a list of stations that could aid in case of a catastrophe. It encourages participating stations to use winlink regulary and train the operators skills. Furthermore: It shall connect likeminded operators to engange in further testing and training.

How does it work in detail?

Operators check in by filling out the form (down below) and sending it to the net control, idealy ccing the backup. Checkin forms are then evaluated once a week, every Sunday 12UTC and the results published to this website. The outcome is a map and a list containing all participating stations, as well as a "callbook" containing vital informations. This callbook is intended for download and offline storage, i shall be used to quickly connect operators in case of an emergency. It is important to note, that operators are not bound to a specific day to check in: Many of us are not able to get on the air regulary or to specific times. I, as an example, are only able to work /p, as i can not put up any antennas at my QTH. Therefore telnet checkins are also accepted, but not encouraged.

What is collected?

Callsign of the operator, First name, Location (Locator or Lat/lon), Bands of operation, modes of operation, times of operation (optional) and further capabilities (optional).

Check in form

The fields are separated by a ";". Spaces do not matter, as long as ";" is not used within the text, only as a delimiter.

Subject: ewn check-in
Message:
CALLSING;FIRST NAME; LOCATION;BANDS;MODES;TIMES;FURTHER NOTES;

Example:
DO1ESB;Erik;JO62;80/15/10;JS8,SSB,VARA HF;MO-WED 19-22;Only portable, times varying;

Example 2 (optionals not filled in):
DO1ESB;Erik;JO62;80/15/10;JS8,SSB,VARA HF;;;
